SUPERNOVA'S

Explosive cosmic wonders shaping the universe in light and energy

Description

A supernova is the spectacular, final explosion of a massive star's life.When a star exhausts its nuclear fuel, it collapses under its own gravity, creating an immense shockwave that releases vast amounts of energy and illuminates the surrounding space. Supernovas are so powerful that, for a short time, they can outshine entire galaxies. They are essential to the universe, dispersing elements like iron and oxygen—vital for forming planets and life—into space, enriching the cosmos with the building blocks for future stars and planetary systems.

Distance From Earth

The closest known supernova to Earth is SN 1987A,
which occurred in the Large Magellanic Cloud,
a satellite galaxy of the Milky Way.
It is approximately 168,000 light-years away from Earth.
Although this event was extremely far,
it was bright enough to be visible with the naked eye
and provided invaluable data to astronomers.
